Moral theory

Here I aim to salvage my hope for the future from its wreckage in the abyssal universe,1 summon all that matters to me 2 and reconstruct a moral cosmos.3

What shape shall I take to rescue the Earth from this flood?
— Mahabharata 4
  1. Hope is wrecked on the electroweak vacuum theory of physics. I aim to salvage it with a subject-object pairing of postulates, namely T interacting with S.
  2. Adding a value postulate R, I infer a ‘moral matter’.
  3. Abstracting from the matter, I recover a pair of forms, test their integrity, and build from them a normative ethic.
  4. William Buck, 1973. Mahabharata. University of California Press, Berkeley.
