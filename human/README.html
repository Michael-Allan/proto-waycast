Being human

Some things I cannot attach to any particular role. Artificial categories no longer apply. At this limit, where all the forms are down “— And Space stares all around —” I see myself just as I was given to be.1

  1. Quote of Emily Dickinson, 1862. Poem beginning ‘It was not Death, for I stood up’. Amherst Manuscript. Fascicle 85, p. 8. (image)
